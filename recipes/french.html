Beef Tacos

Flavorful seasoned ground beef wrapped in warm tortillas, topped with your favorite fixings—quick and delicious for weeknights.

Ingredients

For the Beef Filling

  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef (85% lean)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tbsp taco seasoning (store-bought or homemade*)
  • ½ cup beef broth
  • 1 tsp salt (adjust to taste, depending on taco seasoning)
  • ½ tsp black pepper

For Serving

  • 8 small corn tortillas (6-inch) or flour tortillas
  • 1 cup shredded lettuce or cabbage
  • ½ cup diced tomatoes
  • ¼ cup diced red onion
  • ½ cup shredded cheddar or Monterey Jack cheese
  • ¼ cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges
  • Salsa, guacamole, or sour cream (optional)

*Homemade taco seasoning: 1 tbsp chili powder, 1 tsp ground cumin, 1 tsp paprika, ½ tsp garlic powder, ½ tsp onion powder, ¼ tsp dried oregano, ¼ tsp cayenne pepper (optional)

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add diced onion and sauté for 3-4 minutes until soft.
  2. Add garlic and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant. Add ground beef, breaking it up with a spoon. Cook until browned, about 6-8 minutes.
  3. Drain excess fat from the skillet if needed. Stir in taco seasoning and cook for 1 minute to toast the spices.
  4. Pour in beef broth, stir to combine, and bring to a simmer. Cook for 5-7 minutes until liquid is absorbed and the meat is coated in a thick sauce. Season with salt and pepper if needed.
  5. Warm the tortillas: For corn tortillas, heat a dry skillet over medium heat and cook each tortilla for 10-15 seconds per side until pliable. For flour tortillas, wrap in foil and heat in a 350°F (175°C) oven for 10 minutes.
  6. Assemble tacos: Spoon beef filling onto warm tortillas. Top with lettuce, tomatoes, red onion, cheese, and cilantro. Squeeze lime juice over the top and add salsa, guacamole, or sour cream if desired.
  7. Serve immediately while warm. Enjoy!
