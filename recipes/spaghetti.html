Spaghetti Bolognese

A classic Italian pasta dish with a rich meat sauce, perfect for a comforting family meal.

Ingredients

For the Bolognese Sauce

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, finely diced
  • 2 celery stalks, finely diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef
  • 4 oz (115g) ground pork (optional)
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 cup red wine (optional)
  • 1 (28 oz/794g) can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • ½ tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp salt (adjust to taste)
  • ½ tsp black pepper

For the Pasta

  • 12 oz (340g) spaghetti
  • Water for boiling
  • 1 tbsp salt (for boiling water)
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (for serving)

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onion, carrots, and celery. Sauté for 5-7 minutes until vegetables are soft.
  2. Add garlic and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Add ground beef and pork (if using). Break up the meat with a spoon and cook until browned, about 8-10 minutes. Drain excess fat if needed.
  4. Stir in tomato paste and cook for 1 minute to caramelize.
  5. Pour in red wine (if using) and simmer for 2-3 minutes to reduce slightly.
  6. Add crushed tomatoes, beef broth, bay leaf, oregano, thyme, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine.
  7. Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat to low. Cover partially and cook for 45-60 minutes, stirring occasionally. The sauce should thicken and flavors meld.
  8. While the sauce finishes cooking, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti according to package instructions until al dente.
  9. Drain the pasta, reserving ½ cup of pasta water.
  10. Add the cooked spaghetti to the sauce and toss to combine. If needed, add a splash of reserved pasta water to loosen the sauce.
  11. Serve hot, topped with grated Parmesan cheese.
